Welcome

My name is Kate Whiteman, I am an artist living in Bethesda, Maryland. I believe art is fundamental, so I teach children and young adults, from my home studio here in Bethesda.

This class is not just about art.

It is my goal to nurture and encourage creative thinking, individuality and confidence. Providing tools for self-expression and overall, aid in child development. I hope to share my love for art and pass on my knowledge to influence young minds and make a positive impact.

I offer unique, developmentally appropriate projects to build specific skills and aid in important developmental milestones. Art is proven to benefit mental, social and intellectual health of children, teens and young adults. I believe the process of creating art plays a huge role in self-esteem and developing critical thinking skills for academic success and social functioning. With my individualized assistance, children can learn to nurture themselves, discover and develop their own skills and ideas. Without even noticing it, students will learn emotional regulation tools, such as patience, self-control, tolerance, and adaptability. Providing the tools for success throughout their lives and prepare them for their future.

ABOUT ME:

My motivation and desire for teaching art stems from my own experience with art education. From kindergarten to graduation, I felt I required more attention, more information and more freedom of expression.

I was born and raised here in Bethesda, Maryland, in the same house my father grew up in! I now live just down the road, in a house I share with two of my close friends. I attended Bradley Hills Elementary School, Pyle Middle School, and graduated from Walt Whitman High School in 2011. I am so grateful I get to call Bethesda home and am excited for this opportunity to contribute to my community, by sharing my knowledge, love and appreciation for art.

I graduated from the University of Maryland, College Park, with a Bachelor’s Degree in Studio Art and Art History in 2018.
I now work full-time at Equinox Bethesda, while I continue to create art on the side.

CLASSES:

I teach children and young adults, ages 4 - 19

Elementary and middle school students will be presented with various mixed media projects with simple instruction. They will be introduced to different materials, techniques and styles. Based on students interests and capabilities, they will explore their individuality, and learn about the power of imagination and creativity. The majority of my projects are designed to be completely thought up by, planned out by and executed, all by the student, with loose direction and assistance from myself. While other projects may have specific directions to follow they will still allow students to think out of the box and create freely.

High school and advanced students will also be presented with various mixed media projects, building off background knowledge and interests, and introduced to more challenging concepts and techniques. Students with talent or interest have the opportunity to receive more attention and feedback than they would in school, better preparing them for a college level art course. I specialize in portfolio planning, figure drawing, anatomy, color theory and art history.

POLICIES:

Registration is accepted on a first come first serve basis.
Due to current events classes are limited to 6 students at a time. If there is concern, please reach out for private classes.

At this time a face mask is required in the studio at all times on students and myself. I will be disinfecting all surfaces, tools and materials before and after each class and will keep students and myself at appropriate distances without hindering the learning experience. I will set out all materials and tools we will be using for each class, I ask students to only use their own tools and not to share, as they will all be the same per student. If students wish to use other supplies, they must ask myself and I can get it for them. I will provide aprons for each student. Students may get up from their seat one at a time, to avoid too much contact with each other.

I follow Montgomery County Public School Guidelines for any cancellations and early releases during the school year. Weather is played by ear, and I will contact parents via email the morning of.
